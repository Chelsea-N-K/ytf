Girls4Tech

Tailored for girls, aged 10-13, enrolled in primary school. Girls4Tech uses basic technology and a customized training kit that effectively drives learning and encourages curiosity. In addition to basic coding instruction with Scratch, participants acquire knowledge in financial technology processes including algorithms, digital convergence and cryptography.

Partners: MasterCard, Fundacion Esperanza Pertusa.

3D Africa

Teaches human centered design, computer aided design(CAD) and emerging technology skills to under-educated university students and recent graduates.

Partners: HP, IBM, Makerbot, Autodesk.

Code Clubs

Tailored for girls, ages 6-25. Particpants learn to express themselves with code through games, artwork and learning to code using HTML, CSS and Java. Girls acquire entrepreneurship skills for business and future employment.

Partners: Theirworld.

CS First Clubs

Tailored for students, ages 9-16, and teachers. Aims to build strong foundations in computer science by providing tools and resources that will empower students and teachers with technology skills that will shape their future.

Partners: Google.

eSkills4Girls

Tailored for university-age women and aims to tackle the existing gender digital divide in particular, in low income and developing countries. eSkills4Girls is organized as 2-3 day bootcamps. Students learn emerging technologies such as Android Development, Web Development, Data Science, Robotics and IOT.

Partners: SAP, German Development Agency(GIZ).

Skills For Their Future

Tailored for girls ages 14-20. Aims to empower and unlock economic opportunity for the next generation of marginalized girls and young women, through sociable skills programs relevant in the Fourth Industrial Revolution.

Partners: Theirworld.

Women Entrepreneurs and MVAS

Business skills and financial inclusion training for women entrepreneurs on ways to use mobile value added services(MVAS) to build and expand their businesses.

Partners: MasterCard, Intel, CBFW.
